## Preamble

We have been patient.

We have been patient through the bailouts of 2008, when the gambling debts of private banks were transferred onto public balance sheets without a vote, without consent, without mandate. We were told there was no alternative. We were told civilisation itself hung in the balance. We paid.

We were patient through the austerity that followed — the decade of cuts to hospitals, schools, housing, welfare — presented as fiscal necessity, as the price of responsibility, as the unavoidable consequence of living beyond our means. We had not lived beyond our means. They had. We paid.

We were patient through September 2019, when the overnight lending rate spiked from two percent to ten and the system revealed itself again — not as a market, not as a mechanism for allocating productive capital, but as a permanent extraction machine that requires perpetual rescue. The rescue came. We paid.

We were patient through 2020, when the largest wealth transfer in human history was accomplished under cover of a public health emergency. Billionaire wealth increased by more than half in eighteen months. One hundred million people lost their jobs. The central banks created trillions. The asset prices rose. The wages did not. We paid.

We are no longer patient.

## Article I — We Name the System

The system that governs money, land, and debt in every nation that has submitted to the Washington Consensus, the Basel Accords, the Maastricht Treaty, and the structural adjustment programmes of the International Monetary Fund is not a market. It is a rentier extraction machine.

A rentier does not produce. A rentier extracts. The extraction takes four forms:

**Interest** — charged on money that was created as a ledger entry at the moment the loan was signed, by private banks operating under a licence granted by the state, collecting tribute in perpetuity on principal that never existed before the borrower asked for it. The Bank of England confirmed this in its own quarterly bulletin in March 2014. This is not a conspiracy theory. It is the operating manual of the system.

**Land rent** — collected on the unearned increment of land whose value was created not by the landlord but by the community — by the roads, the schools, the hospitals, the labour of generations who built the neighbourhood and whose descendants can no longer afford to live in it. Henry George named this mechanism in 1879. Nothing has changed except the scale.

**Monopoly rent** — extracted through network effects, patent walls, regulatory capture, and the systematic defunding of public alternatives, so that the population has no choice but to pay the toll. For medicine. For software. For the algorithms that now mediate every transaction between a human being and the economy.

**Financial rent** — the two-percent annual fee on every dollar of managed assets, the carried interest that taxes labour at a lower rate than capital, the dark pools and high-frequency trading that skim microseconds of value from every transaction, the derivatives that allow the same risk to be sold seventeen times while the counterparty exposure accumulates in institutions deemed too large to fail and therefore too large to restrain.

This is the system. It has a name. It is kakistocracy — rule by the worst, selected for and entrenched by the mechanism of the bailout, which rescues failure, rewards recklessness, and punishes prudence at civilisational scale.

## Article II — We Name the Debt

The debt that now presses upon the people of every nation is not sovereign debt.

Sovereign debt, properly understood, is debt incurred by a sovereign people through their legitimate representatives for purposes that benefit that people — roads, hospitals, schools, defence, the institutions of a functioning society. Such debt carries legitimate obligation. We do not dispute it.

What we name and repudiate is odious debt.

The doctrine of odious debt is established in international law. It holds that debts incurred by a regime without the consent of the people, not for the benefit of the people, and where the creditor knew this to be the case, are not binding upon the people. The doctrine was applied in 1898 when the United States repudiated Cuba's debt to Spain. It has been applied selectively and hypocritically ever since, always when convenient to the creditor nations and never when inconvenient. We apply it now. We apply it generally. We apply it to every jurisdiction where it fits. And it fits everywhere.

**Condition one — incurred without the consent of the people.** Not one parliament in the democratic world voted to transfer the bad debts of private banks onto public balance sheets before the transfers were made. The consent was not sought because it would not have been given.

**Condition two — not for the benefit of the people.** The beneficiaries of the 2008 bailouts were the large unsecured creditors of large financial institutions — institutional investors, other banks, pension funds acting as agents of the rentier class, sovereign wealth funds. The depositors were incidental. The workers were not considered.

**Condition three — the creditor knew.** The Bank for International Settlements published the warnings before the crisis. The Bank of England published the warnings. The Federal Reserve published the warnings. The IMF published the warnings and then designed the bailout programmes anyway. They knew. The debt is odious. It does not bind us.

## Article III — We Name the Double-Down

The odious debt has not been repaid. It has been refinanced, compounded, and used as the justification for a second and third round of extraction more brazen than the first.

**Phase one — the crisis.** Deregulate. Inflate the asset bubble. Capture the ratings agencies. Securitise the risk until no single institution can see the whole picture. When the bubble bursts, socialise the losses. Transfer the bad debt to the public balance sheet. Call it systemic necessity.

**Phase two — the austerity.** Use the debt incurred in the rescue as the justification for cutting the public services that might have given people an alternative to debt. Cut housing. Cut education. Cut healthcare. Cut the social wage. The population, stripped of public alternatives, must now borrow to survive.

**Phase three — the asset grab.** With interest rates suppressed to near zero to service the bailout debt, any institution with access to cheap credit can outbid any individual for any asset. Pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, private equity, real estate investment trusts — they buy the housing stock, the commercial property, the infrastructure, the water companies, the care homes.

**Phase four — the next crisis.** The derivatives overhang grows. According to the Bank for International Settlements, notional outstanding OTC derivatives reached $846 trillion in June 2025 — an increase of sixteen percent in a single year, the largest annual rise since before the 2008 crisis. *(Source: BIS OTC Derivatives Statistics, published 8 December 2025)* **[FILED]**. The gross market value stood at $21.8 trillion. These positions cannot be bailed out. The numbers exceed the fiscal capacity of every government on earth combined.

## Article IV — The Great Withholding

We do not propose revolution. The wheel turns and arrives at the same point.

We do not propose reform. The system absorbs reform.

We propose the withholding.

The withholding is the withdrawal of consent from every mechanism by which the rentier class extracts value from the productive economy. It requires no central committee. It requires no vanguard. The conditions coordinate it. The shared condition is intolerable. The withholding has already begun — in Portugal, which stopped twice in six months; in the United States, where May 1st 2026 saw millions withdraw their labour; in every constituency where people have looked at their mortgage, their rent, their student loan, their credit card balance, and asked the question they were never supposed to ask: *who does this money belong to, and why?*

**Withhold your debt service** where the debt is odious — collectively, with legal support, with mutual aid.

**Withhold your custom** from every institution whose business model depends on the rentier extraction. Every pound or dollar that does not go to Amazon is a vote.

**Withhold your attention** from every media institution that naturalises the debt as your obligation, the austerity as your penance, the bailout as your salvation.

**Withhold your legitimacy** from every political institution that presents the management of the extraction machine as the outer limit of democratic possibility.

**Withhold your data** from every platform whose business model is the surveillance of your behaviour for the purposes of more precise extraction.

## Article V — The Jubilee Demand

We make a single demand, stated without hedging.

**Declare the jubilee on all odious debt accumulated since 2008.**

The jubilee is older than capitalism. Leviticus 25. Every fifty years: debts cancelled, slaves freed, land returned. Not as charity. Not as grace. As mechanism — the built-in reset that prevents compound interest from doing what compound interest always does when left unchecked, which is to transfer all the productive capacity of a society to the creditor class within three generations.

The demand is not that the debt be restructured. Restructuring is the creditor's solution. The demand is cancellation.

Cancel the debt incurred to rescue banks that had gambled depositors' money and lost.

Cancel the student debt incurred because public universities were defunded to service previous debt.

Cancel the debt of the global south incurred under structural adjustment programmes designed to open markets for creditor nations while closing the options of debtor nations.

## Article VI — We Reject the Sovereign Default Narrative

They will say: if you cancel the debt, the country defaults. If the country defaults, the bond markets close. If the bond markets close, the hospitals close, the schools close, the lights go out. It will be your fault.

This is a lie.

The sovereigns are the people. The default is theirs — the default of those who govern. They defaulted on the social contract in 2008 when they chose the banks over the people. They defaulted on representative democracy when they handed monetary policy to unelected institutions answerable to creditors.

The bond markets are not a force of nature. They are a mechanism by which the creditor class lends to governments the money that governments could create themselves, at interest. When a government is told it must keep the bond markets reassured, it is being told that the interests of the creditor class take precedence over the interests of the people the government was elected to represent.

The alternative to bond market financing is monetary sovereignty. The money that governments borrow from the bond market at interest is money that governments could create directly through their central banks, as Beardsley Ruml demonstrated in 1946 and as the Bank of England confirmed in March 2014.

We are the sovereigns. We are not defaulting on our obligations. We are refusing to honour obligations that were never legitimately ours.

## Article VII — The Positive Programme

The withholding is not only a refusal. It is a construction.

**Build the housing alternative.** Community land trusts remove land from the speculative market permanently. The land is held in common. The housing is built for occupation, not investment. The model is proven across forty years of practice in Burlington, Vermont; in London; in rural Wales.

**Build the monetary alternative.** Mutual credit schemes allow a community to create its own medium of exchange without reference to a bank, without interest, without the permission of the creditor class. The Mondragon cooperative in the Basque Country has operated for seventy years. The WIR Bank in Switzerland has operated for ninety.

**Build the information alternative.** The dongle. The offline wiki. The federated network with no central server and no single point of deplatforming. WikiTacticalVoting is the constituency-level coordination tool. Every constituency page with a live human on it is a node. The network is the sum of its live nodes.

**Build the political alternative.** Not a party — parties are captured. Not a movement — movements are absorbed. A network of constituency nodes, each sovereign, each coordinating its own withholding and its own construction, connected by shared doctrine and shared tools but not by a central authority that can be compromised.

## Article VIII — To Those Who Will Say This Is Utopian

The charge of utopianism is the rentier's most reliable weapon.

The jubilee is not utopian. It is three thousand years old. It was abandoned not because it failed but because the creditor class grew powerful enough to prevent it.

Monetary sovereignty is not utopian. It is the operating reality of every country that issues its own currency. Japan has run deficits exceeding one hundred percent of GDP for thirty years. The United States has created money without constraint whenever its interests required it — for wars, for bailouts, for quantitative easing.

The utopia is the current system — the belief that an economy organised primarily for the extraction of rent from the productive class by the owning class is stable, sustainable, and permanent. That is the fantasy. The compound interest cannot be paid. The derivatives cannot be unwound. The housing cannot be afforded. The wages cannot service the debt. The arithmetic is not on the side of the rentier. It never was.

## Article IX — The Declaration

We the people who labour and pay and build and raise children and age and die in the economies that have been organised for our extraction, do hereby declare:

That the debt accumulated since 2008 in our name, without our consent, for the benefit of the creditor class, is odious debt under international law and does not bind us.

That the sovereignty of the people over the monetary system that creates money in their name has been stolen and must be restored.

That the land whose value was created by our collective presence and labour belongs to the community and not to the speculator.

That the Great Withholding is our instrument — the collective, coordinated, peaceful withdrawal of our consent, our labour, our custom, our attention, and our debt service from every mechanism of extraction that cannot demonstrate its legitimate claim.

That we will build as we withdraw.

That we will not be moved by the sovereign default narrative, because we are the sovereigns and the default is theirs.

That we demand the jubilee — not as metaphor, not as aspiration, but as policy, as mechanism, as the arithmetic correction that three thousand years of human civilisation has known to be necessary whenever compound interest has been permitted to run unchecked.

And that we will withhold — all of us, at once, from the conditions that coordinate us, which are intolerable — until the jubilee is declared.

**The withholding has begun.**

**The sovereignty is ours.**

**It always was.**
