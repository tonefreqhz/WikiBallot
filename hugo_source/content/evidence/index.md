## Agent R — Rentier Extraction

The $24.4 trillion single-line figure in the prior document had no primary source. Withdrawn. Replaced with three-tier disaggregation.

**Tier 1 — Filed from primary sources**

| Component | Figure | Source | Label |
|---|---|---|---|
| OECD government interest payments | 3.3% of OECD GDP ≈ **$1.6T/year** | OECD Global Debt Report 2026 | **[FILED]** |
| US household debt service ratio | **~11.25%** of disposable income (Q4 2025) | St. Louis Fed / Federal Reserve Z.1 | **[FILED]** |

**Tier 2 — Estimated with transparent methodology**

| Component | Range | Methodology |
|---|---|---|
| Global government interest | **$3.0–3.6T/year** | OECD 3.3% × OECD GDP ~$49T + non-OECD pro-rata |
| Global household interest | **$1.5–2.5T/year** | BIS DSR 17-country sample × global household debt $40T+ |
| Tax haven revenue loss | **$480B–$600B/year** | Tax Justice Network State of Tax Justice 2024 |
| Financial sector fee income | **$1.0–1.5T/year** | AUM estimates × average fee ~1–1.5% |

**Tier 3 — Illustrative construct**

On the basis of the above sourced and estimated components, and drawing on the analytical framework developed by Brett Christophers (*Rentier Capitalism*, Verso, 2020) and Michael Hudson (*J is for Junk Economics*, ISLET, 2017), an illustrative estimate of total annual rentier extraction falls in the range of **$10–18 trillion per year**. This figure is not sourced from a single primary dataset — no such dataset exists. It is constructed from the Tier 1 and Tier 2 components above and should be read as an order-of-magnitude indicator, not a filed statistic.

At $10T low estimate against $4–5T in global social expenditure the ratio is approximately 2:1. At $18T high estimate the ratio is approximately 3.6:1. The direction of the argument does not depend on the precision of the aggregate.
